nine fun facts about cats!

1. Cats Have Three Eyelids: In addition to the upper and lower eyelids, cats have a third eyelid known as the nictitating membrane, which helps protect their eyes and keep them moist.

2. Cats Can Make Over 100 Different Sounds: Unlike dogs, which can make about 10 sounds, cats have a wide range of vocalizations, including meows, purrs, hisses, and chirps.

3. A Group of Cats is Called a Clowder: When cats gather together, they form a clowder, which is a fun term to describe a group of felines.

4. Cats Have a Free-Floating Collarbone: This unique anatomical feature allows cats to fit through tight spaces and helps them with their agility.

5. Cats Can Sprint Up to 50 km/h: When chasing prey or playing, cats can reach impressive speeds, making them one of the fastest animals relative to their size.

6. Only 20% of Orange Cats Are Female: The genetics behind cat coloration means that male cats are more likely to be orange than females, making orange female cats quite rare.

7. Cats Have Fewer Taste Buds Than Humans: Cats have about 473 taste buds, compared to humans' 9,000, and they cannot taste sweetness due to a mutation in their taste receptors.

8. The Oldest Known Pet Cat Was Found in a 9,500-Year-Old Grave: This discovery in Cyprus predates ancient Egyptian depictions of cats by thousands of years, indicating that cats have been companions to humans for a long time.

9. The First Cat in Space Was Named Felicette: In 1963, this French cat was sent into space, making her the first feline astronaut. She survived the journey and contributed to our understanding of space travel.
